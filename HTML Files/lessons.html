In this Lesson we learned what Magnets are, what they can be used for, that some things we would never think are magnetic actually are, and most of all that playing with magnets is fun no matter how old you are!

This Weeks Activities:



Magnetic or Not?


I layed out a bunch of items and you seperated them into two Groups, Those that you thought were Magnetic and those that you did not.

We used the strength of a Magnet to move a Paper Clip through Paint and you made a really cool design.

  • Lesson 2 - Space


@ Uncle Justin's and Aunt Susie's House

(5/30/18)









Content Overview



In This Lesson We Began Learning About Outer Space! We Learned that Gravity is the Force that makes Objects fall and that it is what keeps the planets in orbit around our Sun. We also learned why the moon has a Powdery Surface and what the order of the Planets are.






Click through the arrows to view this lessons content



Vocabulary Terms





Vacuum

Air Resistance

Moon

Gravity

Constellation

Mass

Sun

Solar Still



**Click Here To View And Practice These Flashcards**

This Weeks Activities:



Gravity Drops


I dropped a bunch of different objects from the roof and you guessed which ones would fall quicker.
We learned that unless an object has a lot of air resistance everything falls at the same speed! In a Vaccum aka the moon all objects will fall at the same rate all the time due to no air being present.



Moon Dust Craters


We threw rocks into a container of 'moondust' and learned how craters are formed.

We also learned that the footprints from the moon landing are still there undisturbed because there is no wind!



Order of The Planets


We colored in all of the planets and then spaced them out in the order you guessed they are from the Sun, then we compared it with the actual order. Finally we went outside and layed out the planets according to their current position in the night sky.
We learned that the rocky planets are the closest to the Sun and the Gas Giants are farther away.

Activities Continued...



Solar Still


I prepared a Solar Still using a big plastic bowl, a rubber band, saran wrap, a rock, dirty water, and a small cup.
Using the power of the Sun we learned that through vaporization the water can be made clean and safe to drink. We tasted the water and it was fresh!



Solar Oven


We Built a Solar Oven using cardboard, tin foil, a picture frame and glue.
This was another example of how we can harvest the energy of the Sun and use it in a practical way.



Homework




You Had To Study Your Vocabulary Words and find the answers to questions about your favorite Planet! You also had to cook quesadillas in your Solar Oven!

  • Lesson 3 - Chemistry


@ Uncle Justin's and Aunt Susie's House

(6/27/18)









Content Overview



In This Lesson We Started Exploring What Can Happen Once You Mix Substances Together. We Learned That Certain Substances When Mixed Can Cause Crazy Interactions While Others Don't React At All.

This Weeks Activities:



Borax Slime


We made colored slime out of Borax and Glue!

This was a perfect example of how two completely different substances can be combined to make an entirely new one. And a Super fun one at that!



Rock Candy


We boiled water and added sugar to it until it would no longer dissolve anymore. We also added Kool-Aid to the mix for extra flavor.
We learned that the sugary substance will start to crystallize and grow into a Tasty Treat! .



Black Snake


In the spirit of the 4th of July we took Sugar, Baking Soda, and Lighter Fluid and watched as we caught it on fire!

We learned that heat can be an important part of chemical reactions, and we also learned how the popular firework is made.

Activities Continued...



Eggs in Jars!


We took 4 different mason jars and put a hard boiled egg in each one. We filled them with Coca Cola, Kool Aid , and Vinegar. We left one jar with nothing but the egg and air (This was our control for the experiment).
We learned that acidic substances can cause the shell of an egg to dissolve and leave behind a weird somewhat 'bouncy' inside. Non-Acidic substances such as the air and the Kool-Aid did not produce the same reaction.



Invisible Ink


We wrote out a message on paper with three different liquids. Lemon Juice, Water , and Ginger Pineapple Water.
We learned that the acid in the lemon juice will chemically react with heat and 'burn' our message into the paper for good!



Homework




You Had To Study Your Vocabulary Words, Observe the Eggs and record what happened, finally you had to design your own experiment following the Scientific Method. You Chose to do an Egg Drop!

  • Lesson 4 - Earth's Layers


@ Uncle Justin's and Aunt Susie's House

(09/07/18)









Content Overview



In This Lesson We Started Exploring What's Under Ground. It's not just the dirt you see if you dig a hole in the yard. The Earth has 4 main layers to it, and in this lesson we learned about what they are composed of, how they change over time, and the purposes they serve!

**Click Here To View And Practice These Flashcards**

This Weeks Activities:



Shake Table!


We built a bunch of different buildings out of various materials and then shook them till they broke apart!
We learned that certain types of structures are better at withstanding Earthquakes than others.



Mining


We mined for chocolate chips using "tools" such as toothpicks and paper clips.
We learned that this is similar to how we extract resources from the ground. The ground provides us with almost everything we need to survive and thrive!



Activities Continued...



Pangea Puzzle


We pieced together the continents as they were millions of years ago and discovered some really cool facts.
We learned that the continents all used to be connected together in one huge landmass and we refer to that by the name of Pangea (meaning, "All the Earth").



Volcano


We made a higher powered volcano than the one we did for fun a while back.
We learned that volcanoes can cause Earthquakes and that the Earth's crust slowly gets replaced by volcanic eruptions. We also learned that not all volcanoes are on land, most of them are underwater!

SnowBall Fight!
